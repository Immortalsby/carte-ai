CarteAI Design Direction

Product feeling: Future Dining AI Concierge.

CarteAI should feel like a modern AI dining assistant opened from a QR code,
not a generic restaurant menu and not a chat bot. The customer should be able
to make a decision in seconds with large preset choices.

Visual system:

- Dark-first interface for the customer experience.
- Near-black backgrounds with emerald, mint, cyan, and small violet accents.
- Use restrained glass surfaces, thin borders, soft glows, and scanner-like
  motion to signal AI.
- Keep all food and pricing information clear. Visual effects must never make
  the menu harder to read.
- Avoid warm beige restaurant-template styling on the customer app.
- Avoid generic SaaS dashboard styling on the customer app.

Customer QR page:

- First screen should show the restaurant identity, CarteAI branding, an AI
  scanner/orb element, and preset intent buttons.
- Preset buttons are the primary interaction: first time, under 10 EUR,
  signature, healthy, not spicy, sharing.
- Text input may exist later, but it is secondary.
- Recommendations should feel like an AI analysis card, with clear reason,
  price, ingredients, allergen warning, and budget/health notes.

Owner admin:

- Professional SaaS dashboard, calmer than the customer side.
- Prioritize upload, menu review, edit, publish, QR code, and poster output.
- The admin can use a lighter surface, but it should still inherit CarteAI's
  dark, precise, modern brand.

Poster:

- High-contrast, printable, QR-first.
- Must include the restaurant name, short call to action, QR placeholder/code,
  and carte-ai.link brand exposure.

References:

- Inspired by the AI-platform feeling of MiniMax design language.
- Inspired by the dark cinematic commerce polish of Shopify design language.
- Inspired by the emerald agent-console energy of VoltAgent design language.
- Do not copy brand assets, layouts, or exact visual identity from references.
